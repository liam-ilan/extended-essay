Notes:

  • This is a simulation of two cubes bouncing off of eachother to compute n digits of Pi.
  • By default 5 digits will be computed. To change the number of digits (between 1 and 10) use the url question mark. Eg. https://bouncing-cubes.snowboardsheep.repl.co/?3
  • Collisions are counted between the two cubes, or between cube B and the wall.
  • The masses of the cubes are 1, and 1 * 100^(n-1) where n is the number of digits computed. The floor is frictionless, and all collisions are completly elastic. Additionally, the wall on the left has an infinite mass.
  • Due to this, all collisions conserve energy, and collisions between cubes conserve momentum.
  • Size of cube does not affect the number of collisions, and is for illustrative purposes only.
  • The first 10 digits of Pi are 3.141592653.
